Makeup Trends Of 2023

1. THE YEAR "CLOUD SKIN" CAME ON THE SCENE

Matte makeup made a comeback this year with the "cloud skin" look.
To DIY, swap your cream blush and glossy lips for a mattifying blurring
powder and a seriously glam matte lipstick, and don't forget to prep the skin
with a primer to eliminate excess shine. Embrace the soft focus and blurred
effect and leave the glazed-doughnut skin for another day.

2. THE YEAR OF FAUX FRECKLES

It may be the Meghan Markle effect, but freckles have never had more of
a moment. If your genetics didn't bless you with freckles and you know better
than to spend time outside without sunscreen protection there are still
plenty of ways to achieve a faux-freckle look. Either turn to a long-lasting
liquid pigment, a.k.a.a freckle pen, or book an appointment with a tattoo artist
for a much more permanent effect.

3. THE YEAR THAT OUR LIPS TASTED LIKE CHERRY COLA

The dark-lip-liner craze of the ’90s has found new life
with this glossy, cherry-cola-colored rendition.
All you need to achieve the look is a brown or deep-nude liner
to outline your lips, shading slightly at the corners for
added dimension. Add a high-shine cranberry or deep-red gloss
to the center and voilà, delicious cherry-cola lips..

4. THE YEAR "STRAWBERRY MAKEUP" TOOK OVER TIKTOK

Do you have no clue what that means? You aren’t alone.
Hailey Bieber popularized the "strawberry makeup" look:
a mixture of dark- and light-pink blush
ideally a cream formula
applied across the cheeks and nose where the sun would naturally
hit the skin; a smattering of faux freckles;
a peachy highlighter; and a sheer, glossy lip.

5. THE YEAR WE LEANED INTO FOOD-THEMED MAKEUP

Along with strawberry makeup, beauty influencers
pushed another food-themed look: latte makeup
(which is just a bronze look in shades of brown and nude). The key to
achieving this look is keeping all the colors blended and soft
no harsh contour necessary. It's a foolproof and
undeniably pretty trend that works for any skin tone, budget, or occasion.
